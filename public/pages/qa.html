Dr.'s Q && A


  • What are your current goals?

    I have three main focuses in my life:

    1️⃣ My home, family, and close friends
    2️⃣ The new career path I am pursuing
    3️⃣ My soulmate (i.e., the person I will marry, spend the rest of my life with, and love until the end of my days)

  • What's the deal with communicating through numbers?

    The original explanation is that I'm autistic and my brain is highly analytical (after all, I've spent around 4/5 of my life extremely focused on theoretical physics or software engineering). Therefore, I have a very peculiar way of thinking about reality and expressing my thoughts that is heavily focused on pattern recognition. I think in terms of numbers (and their relationships, such as group associations or Boolean logic rules) quite frequently — they are my main natural language.

    Then, in recent years, as I've been working on my projects (both technical and humanistic), people started to notice that I tend to embed certain numerical rules and symmetries into almost everything I do. Some of my online followers learned these rules and started applying them as well, so we ended up creating a collective natural language based on numerical expression. An entire culture has emerged on top of this game, and you can even draw direct correlations with how Tarot (i.e., the Kabbalah) is structured.

    I'm not going to reveal how our code can be translated, but here are some hints:

    ➡️ 2, 4, 6, and 10 tend to be “soft” communication (each with its own particular meaning)
    ➡️ 5 tends to be on the side of alerting (or, depending on the context, chaos!)
    ➡️ 1 and 8 are related to each other in some sense (can you figure out how?)
    ➡️ 3, 7, and 9 are more mysterious... (and fun?)

    Enjoy playing with us!

  • What does your day-to-day life look like?

    I love routines, and my life is heavily focused on my work and goals.
    My mornings tend to follow the same rhythm:

    ☀️ Wake up, shower, spa
    ☀️ Do some yoga and a headstand
    ☀️ Check the news and play some chess
    ☀️ Have coffee and a protein-based breakfast
    ☀️ Start working (which is what I do most of the day)

    In the evenings, I usually:

    🌙 Have fun → play videogame or VR, read a nice book, watch a nice movie, play some music, dance like no one is watching, cook something healthy && delicious, go shooting some guns, go for a nice walk, do a gym workout, or go for a date with the love of my life
    🌙 Shower, spa, pray, meditate, bedtime

  • What is your weekly schedule? How often do you publish open-source code and writings?

    My weeks tend to look like this:

    ⭐️ I usually work every weekday, and sometimes on weekends. Whenever I have some free time, I update my side projects, such as my writings, Storyteller 13, or Future AI.

    ⭐️ On Saturdays, I try to do something fun outside (like outdoor sports or exploring new places) or simply enjoy my family and friends.

    ⭐️ On Sundays, I try to recharge as well. It's also when I hold my heart-to-heart sessions and publish a personal update on Marina OS, along with a new poem on Luna Capra.

  • Why have you moved so many times?

    During the first part of my life, I used to have this great desire to explore the world and experience all cultures, so I would take any chance to travel that I could.

    In the last decade, because my work as an engineer pays reasonably well and has allowed me to work remotely, I have been kind of a nomad. I have had beautiful houses in many places, but I tended to stay no longer than 1-3 years in each place. At some moments, I was fully nomadic (homeless), staying in beautiful Airbnbs or hotels around the world.

    I have been to 30+ countries, have lived in dozens of cities, and have visited almost every state in the USA.
